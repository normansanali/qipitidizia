Sarah Paulson is an American actress, voiceover artist, and producer whose flourishing acting career has won her many accolades including a Primetime Emmy Award and a Golden Globe Award. She has received critical acclaim with her on-screen performances in shows and movies like Marcia Clark in The People v. O. J. Simpson: American Crime Story, Lana Winters in American Horror Story: Asylum, Bette and Dot Tattler in American Horror Story: Freak Show, Sally McKenna and Billie Dean Howard in American Horror Story: Hotel, Nicolle Wallace in Game Change, Harriet Hayes in Studio 60 on the Sunset Strip, Merlyn Temple in American Gothic, Tammy in Ocean’s 8, Dorothy Olding in Rebel in the Rye, Annie in What Women Want, and Jessica Hayes in Bird Box.

    Sarah Paulson Facts

  • Sarah Paulson made her professional acting debut at the age of 12 with an appearance in an off-Broadway production.
  • The doctors found a melanoma mole on her back when she was 25. It was surgically removed before the spread of cancer.
  • Paulson is the first actress to win 5 major TV awards for her work in a single production. For her role in American Crime Story, she won the Emmy, SAG, Critics’ Choice, Golden Globe, and Television Critics Association.
  • She has been associated with many leading charities and social cause foundations such as UNICEF, Planned Parenthood, and Heifer International.
  • In 2009, she collaborated with Charity Water foundation to provide clean water to a community in Ethiopia. This project was launched on the eve of her 35th birthday.
  • Sarah has two tattoos, one on her back neck featuring stars and starburst, and another on her right foot featuring a starburst.
  • She is severely scared of flying. She only does when there is an absolute necessity and only after personally meeting the flight pilot and getting assurance about their flying experience.
  • She portrayed the character of Marcia Clark in the FX true crime anthology series, The People v. O. J. Simpson: American Crime Story, and this role won her the 2016 Critics’ Choice Television Award, 2016 Primetime Emmy Award, and 2017 Golden Globe Award.
  • In June 2016, she was featured in a video of the Human Rights Campaign as a tribute to victims of the Orlando nightclub terrorist attack. The video has 49 celebrities telling stories of 49 individuals killed in the shooting.
  • Sarah has a dog named Winnie.
  • She was included in the list of “100 Most Influential People in the World” curated by Time magazine in 2017.
  • In 2020, Sarah’s movie Run became the most-watched film in Hulu’s history.
  • She has been friends with Jessica Lange, Felicity Huffman, Elizabeth Reaser, and Amanda Peet.
  • In early 2021, she was injured on the set of American Crime Story and had to wear a cast on her right arm.
  • In August 2022, Sarah was honored with the “Best Actress in a Broadcast Network or Cable Limited or Anthology Series” award at the 2022 HCA TV Awards for her work as Linda Tripp in Impeachment: American Crime Story.
  • In November 2022, Sarah adopted a puppy from Paw Works. She named her dog George.
  • Sarah is close friends with actor Pedro Pascal. They attended the NYU Tisch School of the Arts together. Sarah also supported him financially in his early acting days.
