What Happened to Jacoby Ellsbury?

Jacoby Ellsbury, a former MLB outfielder, has experienced a challenging period in his career due to a series of injuries and contract disputes.  

Injuries and Career Hiatus

Jacoby Ellsbury's career faced a significant setback due to a myriad of injuries, resulting in him missing two full seasons.

His last game was in 2017, and since then, he has struggled with various physical issues, including hip and foot injuries, which have kept him from contributing on the field.

Contract Disputes

Following his prolonged absence from the game, the New York Yankees released Ellsbury in November, leading to a dispute over the payment of $26.142 million owed to him for the 2020 and 2021 seasons. The team contended that Ellsbury violated his contract by seeking unauthorized medical treatment.

The MLB Players Association subsequently filed a grievance against the Yankees, aiming to recoup the money owed to Ellsbury.

This dispute remains unresolved, with the possibility of a settlement or arbitration to determine the outcome.

Current Career Status

Despite the challenges, Ellsbury has expressed his intention to continue his career in 2020. Recent reports indicate that he is "moving toward full workouts" and has resumed baseball activities as he works his way back from his injuries. 

The Yankees, while not intending to include Ellsbury in their plans, still owe him a significant sum of money, with an unresolved debate surrounding his contract and player rights.

Career Highlights and Achievements

Throughout his career, Ellsbury achieved notable milestones and accolades. As a centerfielder, he amassed 1,376 hits and 104 home runs with a batting average of .284. His speed and agility on the field were evident through his impressive record of 343 stolen bases. He's also a 1-time All-Star, won 2 World Series titles, and received both Gold Glove and Silver Slugger awards.

Contract Details and Earnings

Ellsbury signed a lucrative seven-year, $153 million contract with the New York Yankees in 2014, including a no-trade clause and a team option for 2021. His significant earnings and endorsement deals have contributed to his status as one of the world's highest-paid athletes.

 

Jacoby Ellsbury's journey in professional baseball has been marked by both triumphs and tribulations. Despite the challenges posed by injuries and contract disputes, his determination to continue his career reflects his unwavering passion for the game.

As Ellsbury continues to navigate through this challenging phase, his legacy as a talented outfielder and his resilience in the face of adversity remain integral parts of his story. 

Ultimately, the unresolved issues surrounding his contract and the impact of his injuries on his playing ability have left his future in the MLB uncertain.
