Biography/Wiki

Riteish Deshmukh was born on 17 December 1978 (age 39 years; as in 2018) in Latur, Maharashtra, India. He did his schooling from GD Somani Memorial School, Mumbai, and enrolled himself in Kamala Raheja School of Architecture, Mumbai, to attain a degree in architecture. Deshmukh, then, went abroad for a year to practice architecture. He owns Evolution Architectural Design Studio Private Limited (an India-based architectural and interior designing firm). Later, he joined The Lee Strasberg Theatre & Film Institute, California, USA, to study theatre.

One day, while Deshmukh was holidaying with Subhash Ghai in London, Kabir Lal, cinematographer, offered him his first movie. It came as a surprise to him, as he never told anyone about his desire to act.

Family, Caste & Wife

Riteish Deshmukh belongs to a Marathi Hindu Family. His father, Late Vilasrao Deshmukh, was an Indian Politician. Late Vilasrao Deshmukh served as the Chief Minister of Maharashtra for two terms. His mother, Vaishali Deshmukh, is a homemaker.

Career

Riteish began his film career in 2003 with K. Vijay Bhaskar’s “Tujhe Meri Kasam” opposite Genelia D’Souza. In the same year, he appeared in the role of Jaswinder in the film, Out of Control.

In 2004, Deshmukh performed a comic character in the comic thriller, “Masti.” His performance earned him two minor awards. His comedy role in the movie “Masti” established him as an actor. Ritesh’s first successful lead role was in the film “Kya Kool Hai Hum” with Tusshar Kapoor. He also made guest appearances in Darna Zaroori Hai and Namastey London.

Deshmukh, then, went on to star in successful movies like Housefull, F.A.L.T.U, and Double Dhamaal. In 2012, he appeared in Tere Nall Love Hogya opposite Genelia D’Souza. The movie was a commercial hit despite being low-budget. He also starred in the sequels of his movies, Housefull 2, Kya Super Kool Hain Hum, and Grand Masti.

In 2014, Deshmukh made his debut as an antagonist (villain) in the romance-thriller, Ek-Villain. In the same year, he marked his acting debut in Marathi cinema with Lai Bhaari.

In 2013, Riteish produced a Marathi film titled Balak Palak. He also appeared as a judge in the dance reality show, India’s Dancing Superstar.

Apart from acting, the actor has also designed the interiors of Shah Rukh Khan‘s production house, ‘Red Chillies Entertainment’, and Karan Johar’s house at the Union Park (Bandra).

This matter was later politicized and it was believed that the visit was facilitated by Vilasrao Deshmukh in order to cast Riteish Deshmukh in Ram Gopal Varma’s upcoming film based on 26/11 attacks.
