Narco-Saints Actor Hwang Jung Min Bio | Wiki

Hwang Jung Min (born on September 1, 1970) is a 52-years-old Korean actor recognized for his immense contribution to the South Korean entertainment industry. People loved his performance as Byun Jae-wook in Il-Hyeong Lee-directed crime-thriller “A Violent Prosecutor,” which came in 2016.

Besides movies, he has also worked in a few Tv shows, and some of his notable works on TV are “Hush,” “Korean Peninsula,” and “The Accidental Couple (That Fool).”

In 2022, Hwang featured in a Netflix crime drama, “The Accidental Narco,” aka “Narco Saints.” It is a Jong-bin Yoon-directed project where Jung played the part of a prominent character named Jeon Yo Hwan. Famous “Squid Game” actor Park Hae-soo also played an exciting role in this new Korean drama by the OTT giant.

Min’s IMDB profile revealed that he is currently working on two movies, “Kill Bok-soon” and “The Point Men.” The release dates of these two projects are not out, but we will inform you upon getting any further information.

How old is Hwang Jung Min?

On September 1, 1970, Hwang Jung Min | 황정민 (age 52) took birth in Masan, South Gyeongsang Province, South Korea. His father and mother provided him with the care that they could.

Min and his younger brother, Hwang Sang-jun, attended Wolyeong Elementary School and then Masan Middle School. Where the younger brother, Sang-jun, was interested in music, Jung has always been inclined towards acting.

After school, he joined the Seoul Institute of the Arts and studied theatre. After completing college, Min started performing as a theater artist; occasionally, he performed minor roles in short films.

In particular, in the movie “Shiri,” released in 1999, he briefly appeared as a government agent investigating Han Seok-Gyu (with Jang Hyun-Seong) in the movie’s second half.

Who is Hwang Jung Min Married to?

While doing musical theater, Jung met Kim Mi-Hye, an actor. They did a few shows together and became good friends. Slowly they started going out on dates. One day Min told Kim that he loved her. The lady, too, accepted that she had feelings for him. With time their bond got stronger and stronger. In 2004 they married in a private ceremony and have been living happily since that day. The artist couple also has a son named Hwang Sae-Hyun.

Acting Journey of Hwang Jung Min

From 1990 to 1999, Jung did a few movies and mostly played minor roles, but everything changed after he landed the role of Gang-Soo in “Waikiki Brothers.” It was a Soon-rye Yim-directed movie about a music band struggling to grow as artists.

His performance in the “Road Movie” was also terrific; infact, he received the Korean Association of Film Critics Award and the Busan Film Critics Association Award for his performance.

As a movie actor, he has mainly played the role of a simple young man like the male lead in “You Are My Destiny,” but he has been able to transform his acting by brilliantly portraying the role of a cruel and mean gangster boss in Kim Ji- Woon’s “Sweet Life” and a disturbing detective in “A Lifestyle Decision.”

Min has always been a brilliant actor; his performance has always been so elegant, whether it was in shows like “Hush” and “Hostage: Missing Celebrity” or in movies such as “The Spy Gone North,” “The Wailing” and “Deliver Us from Evil,” which is why his fans never miss any of his films and shows.
