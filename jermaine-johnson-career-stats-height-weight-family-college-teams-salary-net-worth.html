Jermaine Johnson: Career stats, height, weight, family, college, teams, salary, net worth

Born on January 7th, 1999, in Eden Prairie, Minnesota, Jermaine Johnson II’s journey to the NFL wasn’t paved with early accolades or privileged opportunities.

It’s a story of resilience, an unwavering pursuit of excellence, and a relentless spirit that transformed him from a community college standout to a rising star in the professional ranks.

Professional Career Stats:

Still in the early stages of his career, Johnson has already carved his name in the Miami Dolphins’ record books, showcasing his explosiveness and versatility in just one season:

2022: 7.5 sacks (led all NFL rookies)

2022: 18 tackles for loss (tied for most among all rookies)

2022: 2 forced fumbles

2022: 1 fumble recovery

These stats paint a picture of a disruptive force, a young pass rusher with a nose for the quarterback and a knack for making game-changing plays.

Height and Weight:

Standing tall at 6 feet 5 inches and weighing 262 pounds, Johnson possesses the ideal physique for a defensive end. His long frame allows him to easily bat down passes at the line of scrimmage, while his explosive first step and powerful hands make him a nightmare for opposing offensive tackles.

College and Draft:

Johnson’s path to the NFL wasn’t conventional. He began his journey at Independence Community College, where he dominated the junior college ranks before transferring to the University of Georgia. His breakout season there, capped by an ACC Defensive Player of the Year award, propelled him to the 2022 NFL Draft, where the Miami Dolphins selected him with the 26th overall pick.

Jersey Number:

Fans easily spot Johnson on the field sporting jersey number 11. This number has become synonymous with his relentless motor, his infectious energy, and his unwavering commitment to being the best player he can be.

Super Bowl Dreams:

While Super Bowl rings remain elusive for now, Johnson’s ambition and leadership qualities are undeniable. He consistently pushes himself to improve, aiming to become a cornerstone of the Dolphins’ defense and ultimately lift the Lombardi Trophy.

Religion and Family:

Johnson’s faith plays a significant role in his life. He openly identifies as Christian, and his strong belief system provides him with unwavering support and strength. He also enjoys a close-knit family, with his parents, Michael and Carolyn Johnson, being his biggest fans and sources of encouragement.

Relationship and Children:

Johnson currently keeps his personal life private. He hasn’t publicly shared any details about a wife or girlfriend, and he doesn’t have any children yet.

Weekly and Annual Salary:

Johnson’s rookie contract reflects his talent and potential. He currently earns a weekly salary of $45,734.62, translating to an annual salary of $2,367,890.24. These figures are a testament to his hard work and dedication, and they are sure to increase as he continues to excel in the league.

Net Worth:

While still early in his career, Johnson’s net worth is estimated to be between $1 million and $2 million.
