Career and Professional Life

Lina Hidalgo ran for County Judge of Harris County in the elections of the year 2018. She ran unanimously in the Democratic Party primary election as well as faced incumbent Ed Emmett in the general election.

She also ran on a platform focused on flood control, criminal justice reform, and increasing transparency and accountability in local government.

Likewise, she defeated Emmett on November 6. She was able to become the first woman as well as the first Latina-elected Harris County Judge.

However, Hidalgo’s victory was considered an upset and it even attracted national attention, with a large and diverse coalition of activists and organizations. This led her to a nearly 19,000-vote victory.

Moreover, she advocated cash bail reform in Harris County.

During the COVID-19 pandemic, she implemented public health measures early to halt the spread of the coronavirus. She also ordered the closure of bars and restaurants in March 2020.

And, in April of the same year, she required Harris County members to wear face masks in public. Republicans at the state and federal levels heavily criticized Hidalgo’s public health measures.
