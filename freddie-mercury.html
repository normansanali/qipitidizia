Discover Freddie Mercury Top Songs and Awards

Freddie Mercury was a British singer/songwriter and musician who died in 1991 with a net worth of USD 50 million (after inflation). Freddie died in 1991 with real estate worth the inflation-adjusted equivalent of $40 million and other marketable assets of $13 million. His net worth was estimated to be between $50 and $60 million.

Mercury is best known as the main singer of the Queen rock band. He was noted for his colorful and theatrical stage demeanor and possessed a four-octave vocal range. Many of Queen’s singles were written by him, including “Bohemian Rhapsody,” “Killer Queen,” “Somebody to Love,” “Don’t Stop Me Now,” “Crazy Little Thing Called Love,” and “We Are the Champions.” Freddie had a solo career in addition to his work with Queen, and he occasionally produced music and guest-starred as a musician (keyboard or vocals) for other artists.

What are some of Freddie Mercury top songs?

Some of Freddie Mercury top songs include:

“Bohemian Rhapsody,” “Killer Queen,” “Somebody to Love,” “Don’t Stop Me Now,” “Crazy Little Thing Called Love,” and “We Are the Champions.”

What are some of Freddie Mercury Awards and nominations?

Some of the late British singer’s top awards include:

WON 1997 British Single of the Year for “Bohemian Phapsody”

Nominated 1982 British Album of the Year for “Greatest Hits”

WON 1980 Outstanding Contribution to Music for “Queen”

WON 1992 Oustanding Contribution to Music for “Freddie Mercury”

WON 1976 Best-Selling British Record for “Bohemian Phapsody”

WON 1987 Outstanding Contribution to British Music for “Themselves”

WON 1992 Best Song Musically and Lyrically for “These Are the Days of Our Lives”

WON 2005 Oustanding Song Collection for “Themselves”
