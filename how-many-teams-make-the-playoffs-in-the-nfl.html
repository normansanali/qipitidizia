How many teams make the playoffs in the NFL?

The National Football League (NFL) is one of the most popular and competitive sports leagues in the world. The NFL playoffs are an exciting and crucial part of the league's season, as they determine the teams that will compete for the coveted Super Bowl title. In this article, we will explore how many teams make the playoffs in the NFL and the structure of the playoff system.

Regular Season Overview

The NFL regular season typically consists of 32 teams, divided into two conferences: the National Football Conference (NFC) and the American Football Conference (AFC). Each conference is further divided into four divisions, with four teams in each division. The regular season spans 17 weeks, during which each team plays 16 games and has one bye week.

Playoff Qualification

At the end of the regular season, a total of 14 out of the 32 NFL teams qualify for the playoffs. The playoff teams are determined through a combination of division winners and wildcard teams.

Division Winners

In each of the two conferences, the team with the best regular season record from each of the four divisions automatically qualifies for the playoffs as division winners. These teams are seeded based on their regular season records, with the division winner with the best record receiving the top seed in their conference.

Wildcard Teams

In addition to the four division winners from each conference, three wildcard teams from each conference also qualify for the playoffs. These wildcard spots are awarded to the three non-division-winning teams in each conference with the best regular season records. These wildcard teams are seeded based on their regular season records and are placed below the four division winners in terms of seeding.

Playoff Structure

Once the 14 playoff teams are determined, the NFL playoffs unfold in a single-elimination tournament format, culminating in the Super Bowl, which is the championship game of the NFL.

Wild Card Round

The playoffs kick off with the Wild Card round, where the three seed and the four seed in each conference host the two wildcard teams. The winners of these games advance to the Divisional Round.

Divisional Round

The Divisional Round consists of four games, with the top seed in each conference hosting the lowest remaining seed from the Wild Card round, and the second seed hosting the other remaining team. The winners of these games advance to the Conference Championship games.

Conference Championship Games

The winners of the Divisional Round games face off in the Conference Championship games, with the victors earning a spot in the Super Bowl as the champions of their respective conferences.

Super Bowl

The NFL season culminates in the Super Bowl, where the champions of the NFC and AFC compete for the Vince Lombardi Trophy, the ultimate prize in American football.

A total of 14 out of the 32 NFL teams make the playoffs at the end of the regular season. These teams compete in a single-elimination tournament, ultimately leading to the crowning of the Super Bowl champion. The NFL playoffs are a thrilling and intense period for football fans, as teams battle it out for the chance to etch their names in football history.
