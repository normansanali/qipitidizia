Is the Frenzied Flame ending evil?

The Frenzied Flame ending in Elden Ring is considered by many to be evil, as it involves the Tarnished embracing the Three Fingers and essentially destroying The Lands Between and everyone in it.

What is the Lord of Frenzied Flame ending?

In the Lord of the Frenzied Flame Ending, the Tarnished embraces the Three Fingers and destroys The Lands Between, potentially causing the destruction of nearly everyone in it.

Why is the Lord of Frenzied Flame ending despicable?

The Lord of Frenzied Flame ending is considered despicable because it involves the Tarnished actively embracing chaos and destruction, ultimately leading to the destruction of The Lands Between and its inhabitants.

What does the Frenzied Flame ending mean?

The Frenzied Flame ending in Elden Ring is a path where the Tarnished becomes the Lord of Frenzied Flame. By embracing chaos and destruction, the Tarnished sacrifices their own body at the Forge of the Giants, potentially leading to the destruction of The Lands Between.

Should I get rid of the Frenzied Flame?

If players want to change the ultimate outcome and ending of their game in Elden Ring, they may want to remove the curse of the Frenzied Flame. Accepting the branding of the Three Fingers will lock players into the Frenzied Flame ending.

What is the point of frenzied flames?

The Frenzied Flame in Elden Ring, although far less present than the Greater Will, influences the Lands Between by bringing chaos and madness. It is connected to the Greater Will, despite being its opposite, and seeks to unleash chaos upon the world.

Does accepting the Frenzied Flame do anything?

In Elden Ring, accepting the Frenzied Flame will lock the player into the Frenzied Flame ending. Removing the curse of the Frenzied Flame is possible, but it requires a long and involved quest.

Does the Frenzied Flame cause madness?

The Frenzied Flame in Elden Ring causes madness in both the user and the target when the yellow flame bursts from the user’s eyes and damages the opponent.

Who is the antagonist in the Frenzied Flame ending?

The antagonist in the Frenzied Flame ending of Elden Ring is the Tarnished, who embraces chaos and destruction by becoming the Lord of Frenzied Flame.

What happens to Melina after the Frenzied Flame ending?

After the Frenzied Flame ending in Elden Ring, Melina may appear in a short cutscene, promising to kill the player. However, the precise outcome may vary depending on the player’s choices and actions throughout the game.

What happens if you refuse Melina in Elden Ring?

If you refuse Melina in Elden Ring, you may miss out on important tools and opportunities for progression in the game. It is generally advised to accept her accord to level up and gain access to a horse.

What’s the hardest ending to get in Elden Ring?

The Lord of Frenzied Flame ending is considered the most difficult to achieve in Elden Ring. It requires undertaking an obtuse and esoteric quest that may not be encountered during standard gameplay.

Which Elden Ring ending is canon?

The default ending of Elden Ring, where the player mends the titular Elden Ring without any alterations, is considered the canonical ending of the game.

What is the longest ending in Elden Ring?

The Age of the Stars ending in Elden Ring is considered the longest and most involved ending. It is the ultimate conclusion to Ranni’s questline.

Is the Dung Eater evil?

The Dung Eater in Elden Ring is considered one of the most evil characters in the game, known for their monstrous actions and sheer horror. Despite this, they have their own questline and ending path.

Why is the Three Fingers evil?

The Three Fingers are considered evil because they serve as the opposing force to the Two Fingers, who represent the Golden Order and the Greater Will. The Three Fingers harbor the power of the Frenzied Flame and are agents of chaos in the game.
