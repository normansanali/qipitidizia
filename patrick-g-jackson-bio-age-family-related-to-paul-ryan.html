Meet Patrick G. Jackson Ketanji Brown Jackson Husband

Ketanji Brown Jackson met her husband Patrick G. Jackson because of her debating skill. As a star in Miami Palmetto Senior High’s speech and debate team, she landed the opportunity to go to Harvard for a debate competition. Despite the winter she called “unbearable,” she met the love of her life while falling in love with the university.

According to NPR, Patrick was Ketanji’s first “serious boyfriend”. Because her husband was a pre-med and math and Ketanji was a government major so she believed that she and her husband were an “unlikely pair” in many respects. But, somehow they found each other and dated continuously for 6 and a half years through the end of college.

Ketanji then earned her law degree and married her husband Patrick on 12 October 1996. Together, they have two daughters, Talia Aenzi (born on 29 June 2001 in Suffolk County, Boston), and, Leila Abeni (16 June 2004 Washington DC).

In the 2017 speech, she also talked about the parenting style. Ketanji shared she and her husband taught their daughters to put work before play and respect all people. She also talked about the difficulties inherent in balancing work concerns with family starting her legal career with a newborn.

Patrick G. Jackson Family

Patrick G. Jackson was born to his parents Francis Gardner Jackson Jr (born on 24 February 1943). and Pamela Graves Hardee (born on 2 May 1944). His parents tied the knots on 12 January 1963. Patrick has an elder brother named Francis Gardner Jackson III born on 17 September 1967 and a twin brother whose name is William Jackson.

His paternal grandparents were Francis Gardner Jackson (4 December 1914 – 31 December 1970) and Jane Matthews (21 August 1916 – 15 July 1993). Patrick’s great-grandparents were James Tracy Jackson Jr. and Rachel Brooks.

His mother was the daughter of Georgina Hazeltine and William Convington Hardee, an American banker, and lawyer who served as lieutenant Commander United States Naval Reserve, 1942-1946. He had graduated with a Bachelor of Arts, Emory University, 1940; Bachelor of Laws magna cum laude, Harvard University, 1943.

In 2017, during the 35th Edith House Lecture, Ketanji stated that her husband was quintessential “Boston Brahmin” whose family could be traced back to England before the Mayflower. But, his family has been in Massachusetts for centuries, he and his twin-brother are 6th generation Harvard.
